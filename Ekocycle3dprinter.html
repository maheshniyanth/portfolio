Ekocycle 3d Printer Rebuilt

Introduction

My brother picked up a broken EkoCycle 3d printer. We decided to fix it so we would have a working 3D printer to use on projects.

The EkoCycle Cube came out in 2014 for $1200. Its claim to fame was the 20% recycled filament that it used. However, it was soon after discontinued due to several reasons. The first was that it required the use of its custom filament that was prone to jamming the nozzle and was expensive. The second was that it was really loud.

Three major things needed to be done:

1. Fix the printer by replacing the electronics.

2. Switch out the extruder and tip so I don't need to rely on the recycled filament and can use PLA.

3. Make it upgradable by switching out the main controller for an Arduino so I can add my own components.

Dismanteling the Printer

The outer plastic frame and electronics were taken off producing a high-quality working aluminum frame. I didn't try to alter anything past this point because everything here was working perfectly. All of the electronic components were taken off other than the stepper motors which still seemed to work.

Electronics

Im using an Arduino Mega, Ramp 1.4 board, DRV8825 Stepper Driver Module, and this power supply I found. The Ramps board made it easy to connect all of the limit switches, fans, and stepper motors to one point. The 1/32 stepping the module also resulted in quieter printing.
